\begin{abstract}
We present an end-to-end pipeline for content- and preference-aware music recommendation grounded in a structured knowledge graph (KG). Starting from the Lakh MIDI Dataset and Million Song Dataset, we align 7,013 tracks with jSymbolic/music21 symbolic features and construct two complementary RDF knowledge graphs (simple and rich variants) encoding 3,668 artists, 645 genres, and 285,037 users, with ontological classes aligned to established vocabularies and Wikidata via OWL semantics. A fully connected autoencoder compresses 1,525 symbolic descriptors per track to a 128-dimensional embedding (final MSE: 0.616). Complementary KG embeddings (RotatE, ComplEx) provide structural initialisation for all entity types across 807,955 triples and 304,110 entities. A Heterogeneous Graph Transformer (HGT)~\cite{hu2020hgt} trained with an intensity-weighted listwise loss incorporating logit adjustment for popularity debiasing achieves Recall@10 of 0.111 and NDCG@10 of 0.071 in a consistent self-evaluation, with 39.9\% catalogue coverage and a low popularity bias of 0.070. Against three baselines, KNN collaborative filtering achieves the strongest Overall Score (0.421 vs.\ 0.209 for HGT and 0.148 for the popularity baseline), driven by its 91.6\% catalogue coverage. Our framework demonstrates how structured musical knowledge and relational semantics can complement behavioural signals, and provides interpretable attention-based explanations for HGT recommendations.
\end{abstract}
