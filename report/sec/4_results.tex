\section{Expected Results}

The expected results of this project include an improvement in recommendation quality achieved by the proposed model compared to the baseline collaborative filtering approach. In particular, the integration of the knowledge graph and musical features is expected to enhance the model’s ability to capture complex relationships between users, songs, and their characteristics, leading to higher performance in terms of evaluation metrics such as Recall and NDCG.

Furthermore, it is anticipated that the proposed approach will provide more diverse recommendations, as the inclusion of content-based features reduces the model’s reliance on popularity-driven patterns commonly observed in standard collaborative filtering methods. This is expected to be reflected in improved coverage and a broader range of recommended items.

Lastly, the expected outcome is better handling of data sparsity and cold-start scenarios, where limited user interaction data is available. \cite{liu2024kgmusic} By incorporating musical features derived from both audio and symbolic representations, the model should be able to recommend relevant items even for users with fewer interactions or for less popular songs. 

Overall, the results are expected to demonstrate that combining knowledge graph structure with rich musical feature representations provides added value to traditional interaction-based recommendation methods.
