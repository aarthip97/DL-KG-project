%% Abstract
\begin{abstract}
We present a content- and preference-aware music recommendation system that
couples a music knowledge graph (KG) with graph representation learning. We
integrate the Lakh MIDI Dataset and the Million Song Dataset, extract
1{,}526 symbolic descriptors per track with jSymbolic, and construct an
RDF/OWL knowledge graph that links users, tracks, artists, genres,
instruments, keys, modes, tempo classes and release decades, with the upper
ontology grounded in Wikidata. On top of this graph we train a stack of
complementary models: a symbolic-feature autoencoder that compresses raw
descriptors into dense track embeddings, two knowledge-graph embedding
models (RotatE and ComplEx) that encode the relational structure, and a
Heterogeneous Graph Transformer (HGT) trained with a popularity-debiased
listwise objective that serves as our main recommender. We compare against three baselines---a popularity prior, user--user KNN
collaborative filtering ($k$=6), and a two-stage gradient-boosted
content/CF hybrid---on a shared stratified user-level split of 284{,}864
users and 6{,}054 tracks (1.94M training interactions). Results at $K$=10
show that KNN-CF achieves NDCG of 0.106 and 91.5\% catalogue coverage with
a composite Overall score of 0.420, tripling the popularity baseline (0.134)
through diversity gains rather than raw accuracy improvements. The XGBoost
hybrid reduces popularity bias to 0.032 (lowest of any model) but at a large
accuracy cost (NDCG 0.015), highlighting the limits of content-only candidate
generation without collaborative signals.
